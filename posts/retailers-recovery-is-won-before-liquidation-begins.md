Most retailers are trying to fix recovery at the wrong end of the pipe.

They focus on auctions, liquidators, and downstream pricing—after the damage is already done.

Here’s the reality:

**Recovery is determined upstream.**

By the time product hits liquidation, 70–90% of its outcome is already locked in.

What actually drives recovery?

* **Return policy design** → what comes back, in what condition
* **Intake and triage speed** → days vs. weeks changes value materially
* **Grading discipline** → A/B/C only works if it’s enforced, not aspirational
* **Disposition rules** → pre-set channel paths beat ad hoc decisions every time
* **Data integrity** → bad manifests = discounted bids, every time

If these are loose, no marketplace, broker, or buyer network will save you.

You don’t have a pricing problem.
You have a **process control problem**.

Operators who get this right:

* Move product in days, not weeks
* Protect condition before it degrades
* Route inventory intentionally, not reactively
* Price off **realized recovery curves**, not MSRP fiction

Result: +300–800 bps recovery and materially faster cash conversion.

If you want to improve recovery, stop negotiating harder downstream.

Start engineering it upstream.
