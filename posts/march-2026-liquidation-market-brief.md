Three things are reshaping the wholesale liquidation landscape right now, and smart buyers and sellers need to be positioned ahead of each one.

## The Tariff Ruling: Real Money, But It Will Not Come Fast

A federal judge recently ordered U.S. Customs and Border Protection to refund IEEPA tariffs and stop collecting them on imports still in the liquidation process. For liquidators and secondary market buyers, this matters more than the headline suggests.

Here is why. Retailers who front-loaded inventory in late 2025 to beat tariff implementation are now sitting on excess stock they over-purchased. They bought ahead of demand to protect against cost increases. Now that the tariff situation is shifting, the urgency to hold that inventory is lower — and the pressure to move it is real.

The result: expect more forced selling in Q2 2026. Bigger lots. More motivated sellers. More negotiating leverage for buyers who are positioned and capitalized to move fast.

The opportunity is coming. The timeline is not instantaneous. Do not expect it to hit all at once in April. This is a Q2 story that builds across the quarter as retailers assess their positions and make disposition decisions. Be ready. Have capital available. Know your channel capacity before the volume arrives.

## Lot Sizes Are Growing. Volatility Is Increasing. Discipline Wins.

This is not new for experienced buyers, but it is worth stating clearly: the days of relatively uniform lot quality and predictable pricing are increasingly behind us.

Supply is rising across the board. Lot sizes are getting bigger. And when lot sizes grow and quality dispersion widens, pricing becomes more volatile, not less. You will see great lots and terrible lots in the same category in the same week. Reserve pricing will be inconsistent. Condition grade claims will vary.

There is one response to this environment: discipline. Specifically, pricing discipline.

If you are still pricing loads off MSRP, you are going to get burned in this market. MSRP-based pricing has always been a lazy shortcut, but in a high-volatility environment it becomes a direct path to losses. The only pricing that matters right now is current verified retail pricing — what the item actually sells for in the relevant resale channel today — combined with a real condition mix assumption and your full cost stack.

Buyers who have that model built will outperform. Buyers who are guessing will have a difficult Q2.

## DIY and Home Improvement: Bid With Confidence

Not everything is a cautionary story. Spring demand is pulling tool, hardware, and home improvement categories ahead of the pack right now, and the data supports it.

Harvard's Joint Center for Housing Studies remodeling data confirms continued early 2026 growth in home improvement spending. Home Depot's inventory build — up over 10% YoY — combined with spring project demand means faster turns and stronger recovery in DIY-adjacent categories compared to the rest of the secondary market.

If you are buying returns or overstocks in tools, hardware, garden, and home improvement right now, bid with more confidence than you would in a flat market. The turns are faster. Recovery is stronger. And spring demand gives you a natural window to move inventory efficiently.

This is not a permanent condition — summer will tell a different story. But for Q2 2026 buying, home improvement is one of the clearest bright spots in the liquidation market.

## How to Position Right Now

**Be ready for Q2 forced selling** out of the tariff inventory overhang. Have capital and channel capacity available before the volume arrives.

**Price everything off current verified retail**, not MSRP. Build your condition mix assumptions from real data, not optimistic grades.

**Lean into DIY and home improvement** while spring demand supports faster turns and better recovery.

**Stay disciplined in broadline GM.** The supply is real, but so is the quality dispersion. Know what you can process and price accordingly.
